The Problem

For service businesses — consultants, bookkeepers, coaches, agencies — the moment a prospect submits a contact form is the highest-intent moment in the entire sales funnel. And most businesses blow it.

The follow-up is manual. Someone has to see the notification, copy the prospect's name, write something personalized, and remember to follow up again in two days if there's no response. Response times vary. Follow-ups get forgotten. The process doesn't scale — and it doesn't have to be this way.

Before
  • Hours to first response (dependent on someone checking email)
  • 5–10 minutes of manual work per new lead
  • Generic reply templates that don't reference the prospect's goal
  • Follow-ups missed — no system, just memory
  • Leads scattered across inboxes with no central log
After
  • Personalized email sent in under 30 seconds
  • 0 minutes of manual work per lead
  • Every email directly references the prospect's stated goal
  • 48-hour follow-up fires automatically if no booking
  • Every lead logged to a Google Sheet in real time

How It Works

The system runs entirely in n8n and triggers the moment a form is submitted. No polling. No delays.

1
Form submitted (Tally) Prospect fills out the intake form — name, business, industry, and their specific goal. Tally fires a webhook to n8n instantly.
2
Lead logged to Google Sheets n8n appends a row: name, email, business, goal, timestamp, and status. Single source of truth. No manual data entry.
3
Claude writes the welcome email The prospect's goal is passed to Claude via the Anthropic API. Claude generates a personalized email in the founder's voice — not a template. The email specifically addresses what the prospect wrote in the form.
4
Email sent via Gmail API (<30 seconds from form submit) n8n sends the Claude-generated email through Gmail. The prospect receives a response that reads like it was written by a human who actually read their submission.
5
48-hour follow-up (conditional) n8n sets a 48-hour wait. If the prospect has booked a Calendly call, the follow-up is skipped. If not, one follow-up email goes out automatically — then the sequence stops.
